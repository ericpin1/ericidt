WHO IS LOGIC?

Sir Robert Bryson Hall II (born January 22, 1990) is a retired American rapper, songwriter , and record producer, as well as a streamer and author, professionally known as Logic. Logic released six studio albums as a rapper, and earned two Grammy nominations.


Logic began his music career in 2010, releasing the mixtape Young, Broke & Infamous; with his Young Sinatra mixtape series, he gained attention, leading to his third instalment, Young Sinatra: Welcome to Forever (2013), helping Logic sign a recording contract with Def Jam Recordings. The first two studio albums by Logic — Under Pressure (2014) and The Amazing True Story (2015) — both peaked in the top five on the US. Billboard 200 and were gold-certified.

Logic gained mainstream success with Everybody (Logic's Third Official Studio Album) in 2017; the album charted to number one in the United States and was certified platinum, while its lead single, "1-800-273-8255," reached number three on the United States. Billboard Hot 100, and was an international top ten hit. He also launched the commercial mixtape series Bobby Tarantino. His fourth album YSIV (2018) and fifth album Confessions of a Dangerous Mind (2019) achieved commercial success, with the latter featuring the single 'Homicide' which peaked at number five on the Hot 100. After releasing his final album No Pressure (2020), he began a streaming career.


Before all the fame, lets take a step back in to the early life of Logic or Sir Robert Bryson Hall II. Sir Robert Bryson Hall II was born on January 22, 1990. He was born to a white mother and Robert Bryson Hall, an African-American Maryland resident. He has seven half-siblings between his father and mother. Logic spent most of his childhood in the neighbourhood of West Deer Park, in Gaithersburg, Maryland. His father had an addiction to cocaine, and his mother suffered from alcoholism. Although his father was initially absent in his childhood, owing to his burgeoning rap career, Logic was able to reconnect with Hall. Logic observed his brothers cultivating and selling crack cocaine to "block-wide abusers" during his early teenage years, including his father. Logic maintains that he accurately knows how to manufacture and produce crack cocaine following these experiences.

Logic's inspiration for rap has been present for almost his entire life. At the age of 13 to be exact, Logic became interested in rap and hip hop after watching the movie, Kill Bill, famously directed by Quentin Tarantino. RZA, a member of the hip hop group Wu-Tang Clan, produced the score for the film. Thus Logic, being heavily inspired by the movie started listening to the group after this, and soon expressed a deep affection and connection for hip hop.In 2009, Logic performed under the stage name "Psychological" in which he stated his choice of nickname as "one name that really stuck [with me]. I just loved this word because it was about the mind and I knew that's what I wanted my music to consist of; something that really challenges the mind".
